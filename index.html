Week 5 of 14 · June 29, 2026

Tell Me About Yourself and Your Unique Value Proposition

Week four set the strategy. This week turns it into words you can say out loud: a Tell Me About Yourself answer built on Personal, Relevance, Pursuit, drafted with you in Reimagine and pointed at the lane you picked.

Highlights from the conversation:
  • Reimagine is where this week's teaching becomes work. The call gives you the formula for Tell Me About Yourself. Reimagine's Bridge Story walks that exact formula with your own material and drafts a version tailored to your lane or a specific role.
  • The Bridge Story follows Personal, Relevance, Pursuit. A key trait or experience, how it played out in your career, and what you are looking for now. Reimagine builds the first draft from your brand inputs, then you shape it until it sounds like you.
  • Tailor it to your lane. Whether you are on Familiar Ground, moving as an Industry Insider, or heading toward Work That Matters, Reimagine points the story in that direction so it lands as relevant instead of generic.
  • Bake a cake rather than pick one ingredient. Your experiences are eggs, flour, sugar. The question is not which one to be. Blend them into one brand that is yours alone, and let Reimagine help you say it in a sentence or two.
  • Networking and interviewing want different versions. A networking introduction stays broader about your direction. An interview answer gets specific to the role in the room. Reimagine can produce both from the same brand.
  • The draft gets better as you correct it. The first pass will miss in places. Tell Reimagine what is off, and the second or third version usually lands. The new thumbs up or down after each response feeds that loop.
  • Use it to build evidence, not to second-guess yourself. When a role feels like a stretch, Reimagine helps you weigh the fit and point to proof, so confidence rests on what you have done.
Your work this week:
  • Generate your Bridge Story in Reimagine. Open your personal brand, then let it draft your Tell Me About Yourself. Plan about twenty minutes.
  • Tailor it to your lane or a live role. Point the story at Familiar Ground, Industry Insider, or Work That Matters, or at a specific job description.
  • Take the Affintus assessment if your inputs are thin. Free, about fifteen minutes, paste the results into Reimagine to enrich the brand.
  • Practice out loud and leave feedback. Read it aloud until it sounds like you, and use thumbs up or down to sharpen the next draft.

● Week 3 The Four Cs of Personal Brand Recap ▾

The four Cs of personal brand: convictions, clarity, confidence, and contagiousness. Convictions are the values you would be willing to be fired over, clarity turns them into a focused message, confidence rests on evidence you can point to, and that energy spreads to the people deciding whether to say yes. A clear brand beats a long list of skills, and putting your accomplishments in numbers gives it something concrete to stand on.
